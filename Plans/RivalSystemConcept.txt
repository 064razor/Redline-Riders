Rival System Concept

Core idea:
Named racers are people in the world.
Rivals are people with rules.

Named racers:
- Can cameo in regular races when the player's car looks like a reasonable matchup.
- Keep consistent names, cars, visual styles, and rough build identities.
- Do not all need to be full rivals or story-important characters.
- Can appear in tournament/event lineups for flavor and continuity.

Rivals:
- Are named racers with extra structure and identity.
- Can appear in tournament events.
- Can be challenged from a Rival menu.
- Should be challenged on their own terms, not as generic rematches.

Rival menu ideas:
- Show discovered rivals as cards.
- Hide undiscovered rivals as locked silhouettes or unknown racers.
- Show rival car, record against the player, preferred track type, and challenge terms.
- Let the player challenge a rival at will only if the terms are met.
- If terms are not met, explain why.

Possible rival terms:
- Required track length.
- Required or banned aspiration type.
- Car performance range.
- Specific class or car type later.
- Special payout or rematch rules.

Examples:
- Cruz: clean Maru builds, beginner-to-intermediate 1/4 mile races.
- Rocket: heavier muscle builds, longer tracks, big-power pressure.
- Pinky: NA-only challenge identity.
- Mako: aero and top-speed focused long-track races.
- Cyber Riders: named crew that can appear together in themed events or separately as cameos.

Implementation direction for later:
1. Rival profiles.
2. Rival records per save.
3. Named racer cameo pool for regular races.
4. Rival menu.
5. Rival challenge rules.
6. Rival event/tournament integration.
